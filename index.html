Rites of passage and other ceremonies

African communities have many traditions when it comes to rites of passage.

This is a ritual of passage which occurs when an individual passes one group to another.

  1. Initiation

    2. This is a rite of passage where a group of same age undergoes.It may be circumcision,removal of lower teeth.

    Some communities used to circumcise both boys and girls and when people of same age where circumcised together they joined same age-set.

  2. Birth rites

    3. In some african countries birth takes place in the house of the expectant mother. In South Africa birth takes place on a grinding stone as a symbol of womans hardwork. In Ethiopia the mother does it alone in the bush and returns home with the child after delivery while in in kenya, when a gikuyu woman gives birth she screams five times if the child is a boy and four times if its a girl.

Other ceremonies include,

  1. Kweta ceremony

    2. Its a south african ceremony that is for young boys for them to prove man-hood

  2. Bull jumping

    3. Inorder for young men to prove their man-hood, they have to jump and land on the back a bull. This is usually done when they are nude

  3. Lip stretching

    4. When a girl becomes a teenager in the Surma tribe of Southern Sudan, she begins the process of lip stretching. The girl has her bottom teeth removed to make space for a lip plate, which is increased in size annually.
